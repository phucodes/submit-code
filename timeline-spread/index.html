• Oct. 4, 1957

    First Artificial Satellites

    A modified R-7 two-stage ICBM launches the satellite Sputnik 1 from Tyuratam. The Space Race between the Soviet Union and the United States begins.

  • Nov. 3, 1957:

    Laika

    The Soviet Union launches Sputnik 2 with the first living passenger, the dog Laika, aboard.

  • Jan. 31, 1958

    First American Satellites

    Explorer 1, the first satellite with an onboard telemetry system, is launched by the United States into orbit aboard a Juno rocket and returns data from space.

  • Oct. 7, 1958

    NASA

    NASA Administrator T. Keith Glennan publicly announces NASA's manned spaceflight program along with the formation of the Space Task Group, a panel of scientist and engineers from space-policy organizations absorbed by NASA. The announcement came just six days after NASA was founded.

  • Jan. 2, 1959

    Luna 1

    The U.S.S.R. launches Luna 1, which misses the moon but becomes the first artificial object to leave Earth orbit.

  • May 28, 1959

    The first Primates in Space

    The United States launches the first primates in space, Able and Baker, on a suborbital flight.

  • Aug. 7, 1959

    First Photograph from Space

    NASA's Explorer 6 launches and provides the first photographs of the Earth from space.

  • Oct. 24, 1960

    Baikonour Incident

    To rush the launch of a Mars probe before the Nov. 7 anniversary of the Bolshevik Revolution, Field Marshall Mitrofan Nedelin ignored several safety protocols and 126 people are killed when the R-16 ICBM explodes at the Baikonur Cosmodrome during launch preparations.

  • Feb. 12, 1961

    Verona to Venus

    The Soviet Union launches Venera to Venus, but the probe stops responding after a week.

  • April 12, 1961

    First Man in Space

    Yuri Gagarin becomes the first man in space with a 108-minute flight on Vostok 1 in which he completed one orbit.

  • May 5, 1961

    Mercury Freedom 7

    Mercury Freedom 7 launches on a Redstone rocket for a 15-minute suborbital flight, making Alan Shepard the first American in space.

  • May 25, 1961

    JFK's Before the End of the Decade

    In a speech before Congress, President John Kennedy announces that an American will land on the moon and be returned safely to Earth before the end of the decade.

  • Oct. 27, 1961

    Saturn 1

    Saturn 1, the rocket for the initial Apollo missions, is tested for the first time.

  • Feb. 20, 1962

    First American manned orbital flight

    John Glenn makes the first U.S. manned orbital flight aboard Mercury 6.

  • June 14, 1962

    European Response

    Agreements are signed establishing the European Space Research Organisation and the European Launcher Development Organisation. Both eventually were dissolved.

  • Aug. 27, 1962

    First successful interplanetary flyby

    Mariner 2 launches and eventually performs the first successful interplanetary flyby when it passes by Venus.

  • Sept. 29, 1962

    First non-US or non-Soviet launch

    Canada's Alouette 1 launches aboard a NASA Thor-Agena B rocket, becoming the first satellite from a country other than the United States or Soviet Union.

  • June 16, 1963

    First Woman in Space

    Valentina Tereshkova becomes the first woman to fly into space.

  • Oct. 12, 1964

    Voskhod

    The Soviet Union launches Voskhod 1, a modified Vostok orbiter with a three-person crew.

  • March 18, 1965

    First Spacewalk

    Soviet cosmonaut Alexei Leonov makes the first spacewalk from the Voskhod 2 orbiter.

  • March 23, 1965

    First seasoned traveller

    Gemini 3, the first of the manned Gemini missions, launches with a two-person crew on a Titan 2 rocket, making astronaut Gus Grissom the first man to travel in space twice.

  • June 3, 1965

    Second, but still the first

    Ed White, during the Gemini 4 mission, becomes the first American to walk in space.

  • July 14, 1965

    First Successful Mars flyby

    Mariner 4 executes the first successful Mars flyby.

  • Feb. 3, 1966

    Luna to Luna

    The unmanned Soviet spacecraft Luna 9 makes the first soft landing on the Moon.

  • March 1, 1966

    Venera 3

    The Soviet Union's Venera 3 probe becomes the first spacecraft to land on the planetVenus, but its communications system failed before data could be returned.

  • March 16, 1966

    The First Docking

    Gemini 8 launches on a Titan 2 rocket and later docks with a previously launched Agena rocket — the first docking between two orbiting spacecraft.

  • April 3, 1966

    First Spacecraft to orbit the Moon

    The Soviet Luna 10 space probe enters lunar orbit, becoming the first spacecraft to orbit the Moon.

  • Jan. 27, 1967

    Apollo 1 Incident

    All three astronauts for NASA's Apollo 1 mission suffocate from smoke inhalationin a cabin fire during a launch pad test.

  • April 23, 1967

    Soyuz 1 Fatality

    Soyuz 1 launches but myriad problems surface. The solar panels do not unfold, there are stability problems and the parachute fails to open on descent causing the death of Soviet cosmonaut Vladimir Komarov.

  • Oct. 11, 1968

    Smile and Wave

    Apollo 7, the first manned Apollo mission, launches on a Saturn 1 for an 11-day mission in Earth orbit. The mission also featured the first live TV broadcast of humans in space.

  • Dec. 21, 1968

    First Manned Mission to Orbit the Moon

    Apollo 8 launches on a Saturn V and becomes the first manned mission to orbit the moon.

  • Jan. 16, 1969

    First in-orbit crew transfer

    Soyuz 4 and Soyuz 5 rendezvous and dock and perform the first in-orbit crew transfer.

  • March 3, 1969 to May 22, 1969

    American Lunar Module Tests

    Lunar Landing Module tests were carried during Apollo 9 and 10.

  • July 20, 1969

    First Human on Another Planet

    Six years after U.S. President John F. Kennedy's assassination, the Apollo 11 crew lands on the Moon, fulfilling his promise to put an American there by the end of the decade and return him safely to Earth.

  • Nov. 26, 1969

    The third and the Non-aligned

    France launches its first satellite, Astérix, on a Diamant A rocket, becoming the third nation to do so.

  • Feb. 11, 1970

    Ohsumi

    Japan's Lambda 4 rocket launches a Japanese test satellite, Ohsumi into orbit.

  • April 13, 1970

    Apollo 13 Aborted

    An explosion ruptures thecommand module of Apollo 13, days after launch and within reach of the moon. Abandoning the mission to save their lives, the astronauts climb into the Lunar Module and slingshot around the Moon to speed their return back to Earth.

  • April 24, 1970

    Great Leap Forward

    The People's Republic of China launches its first satellite, Dong Fang Hong-1, on a Long March 1 rocket, becoming the fifth nation capable of launching its own satellites into space.

  • Sept. 12: 1970

    First Automated Lunar Retrieval

    The Soviet Union launches Luna 16, the first successful automated lunar sample retrieval mission.

  • April 19, 1971

    First Space Station

    A Proton rocket launches the first space station, Salyut 1, from Baikonur.

  • June 6, 1971

    Soyuz 11

    Soyuz 11 launches successfully, docking with Salyut 1. The three cosmonauts are killed during re-entry from a pressure leak in the cabin.

  • Oct. 28, 1971

    Prospero

    The United Kingdom successfully launches its Prospero satellite into orbit on a Black Arrow rocket, becoming the sixth nation capable of launching its own satellites into space.

  • Nov. 13, 1971

    Total Orbiter

    Mariner 9 becomes the first spacecraft to orbit Mars and provides the first complete map of the planet's surface.

  • March 3, 1972

    The First Spacecraft to leave the system

    Pioneer 10, the first spacecraft to leave the solar system, launches from Cape Kennedy, Fla.

  • Dec. 19, 1972

    Last Mission to the Moon

    Apollo 17, the last mission to the moon, returns to Earth.

  • May 14, 1973

    America's first space station

    A Saturn V rocket launches Skylab, the United States' first space station.

  • March 29, 1974

    First Mercury flyby

    Mariner 10 becomes the first spacecraft to fly by Mercury.

  • May 31, 1975

    EAS

    The European Space Agency is formed.

  • July 20, 1976

    The Red Planet

    The U.S. Viking 1 lands on Mars, becoming the first successful Mars lander.

  • Sept. 1, 1979

    First Saturn flyby

    Pioneer 11 becomes the first spacecraft to fly past Saturn.

  • Dec. 24, 1979

    Europe joined the race

    The French-built Ariane rocket, Europe's first launch vehicle, launches successfully.

  • April 12, 1981

    Columbia

    Space Shuttle Columbia lifts off from Cape Canaveral, beginning the first space mission for NASA's new astronaut transportation system.

  • June 18, 1983

    And the third woman in space

    Sally Ride aboard the Space Shuttle Challenger becomes the first American woman in space.

  • Feb. 7, 1984

    First ever untethered space walk

    Astronauts Bruce McCandless and Robert Stewart maneuver as many as 328 feet (100 meters) from the Space Shuttle Challenger using the Manned Maneuvering Unit, which contains small thrusters, in the first ever untethered spacewalks.

  • Sept. 11: 1985

    First comet flyby

    The International Cometary Explorer, launched by NASA in 1978, performs the first comet flyby.

  • Jan. 28, 1986

    Challenger Incident

    Challenger explodes 73 seconds after launch, grounding the shuttle fleet for more than two years.

  • Feb. 20, 1986

    Peace

    The Soviet Union launches the Mir space station.

  • Nov. 15, 1988

    Buran and Only

    The Soviet Union launches its Buran space shuttle on its only flight, an unpiloted test.

  • April 25, 1990

    Hubble Space Telescope

    The Space Shuttle Discovery releases the Hubble Space Telescope into Earth orbit.

  • December 25, 1991

    United Forever in Friendship and Labour

    On December 25, 1991, the Soviet hammer and sickle flag lowered for the last time over the Kremlin, thereafter replaced by the Russian tricolor.
